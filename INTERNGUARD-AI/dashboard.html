How to Spot a Scam

üí∞
Never Pay to Apply
Legitimate internships and jobs never require you to pay any registration, training, or security deposit fee. Any such request is an immediate red flag.
üìß
Verify the Email Domain
If a company recruiter contacts you from a Gmail, Yahoo, or Hotmail address, be very cautious. Legitimate companies use their own domain (e.g., hr@company.com).
üåê
Check the Website URL
Always verify the company's official website. Watch out for suspicious TLDs (.xyz, .tk), URL shorteners, or URLs that don't match the company name.
üîç
Research on LinkedIn
Search for the company and the recruiter on LinkedIn. A real company will have a verified company page with employees and an established presence.
üö®
Beware of Urgency
Phrases like "Apply IMMEDIATELY," "Limited seats," or "Offer expires today" are classic pressure tactics used by scammers to prevent you from doing due diligence.
üîí
Guard Personal Data
Never share Aadhaar number, PAN card, bank account details, or passport information with a recruiter until you have verified the company is legitimate and you have accepted an offer.
